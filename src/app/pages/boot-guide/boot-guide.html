Advanced Troubleshooting

üîã Battery Degradation

Very common issue: Moxie robots use lithium batteries that degrade over time. If your Moxie won't boot even after long charging, the battery may no longer hold sufficient charge.

Solution: Keep Moxie permanently plugged in during use. Many users successfully run Moxie this way as a stationary device.

üîÑ Factory Reset

If Moxie is stuck in a boot loop (constantly restarting), a factory reset may help. Note: This will erase all data on Moxie.

How to reset: Hold power button for 10+ seconds until Moxie shuts down completely. Wait 30 seconds, then power on normally.

üì° Network Connection

Once Moxie shows the QR code, it's trying to connect to a server. With OpenMoxie, this will be your local computer instead of Embodied's cloud servers.

Next step: Follow the OpenMoxie setup guide to connect Moxie to your local server. Download the app from the home page.

‚ö° Power Supply Requirements

Moxie requires a stable 5V power supply with at least 2A current. Some USB chargers don't provide enough power for the boot process.

Recommended: Use a high-quality USB wall adapter (like an iPad charger) or connect to a powered USB 3.0 port on your computer.

Complete Moxie Robot Boot Guide

Booting up your Moxie robot can sometimes be challenging, especially if you're experiencing common issues like no QR code, stuck blue chest light, or battery problems. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about starting your Moxie robot and preparing it for OpenMoxie 2.0 setup.

Understanding Moxie's Boot Process

When you power on your Moxie robot, it goes through several stages before reaching the ready state (QR code display). The chest light provides visual feedback about the current boot stage. Understanding these Moxie chest light indicators is essential for troubleshooting boot issues.

Common Moxie Boot Problems

The most frequent Moxie robot startup issues include battery degradation, insufficient power supply, and boot loops. These problems affect both stock Moxie and robots being set up with OpenMoxie. Battery issues are particularly common in older Moxie units.

Getting Ready for OpenMoxie Setup

Once your Moxie robot displays the QR code, it's ready to connect to a server. With OpenMoxie 2.0, this connection goes to your local computer instead of cloud servers, giving you complete privacy and control. The setup process is straightforward once Moxie reaches this ready state.
